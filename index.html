Facial Recognition in the News

the top terms used to talk about facial recognition in English-language news sources

By: Rahul Bhargava

This sketch looks at how facial recognition was covered in English-language online news in the US, UK, India and Nigeria between January of 2019 and March of 2020.

Inspired by The Density Design lab at the Politecnico Milan, which does wonderful work researching and teaching how to visualize complex phenomena, this sketch responds to a recent project from their Final Synthesis Design Studio course. That project created an interesting visual — and produced some initial findings — on how different people on global social media networks discuss facial recognition. Their second research question was particularly interesting to me: "In which terms are people talking about facial recognition on the most popular social media platforms in U.S. (and other English speaking countries), China, Italy and India?"

I was interested to read their findings, because my colleague Joy Buolamwini, a PhD student in our group, the MIT Center for Civic Media, founded the Algorithmic Justice League and has done some of the most important early work on this topic. My own work includes Media Cloud, a set of tools and processes for studying attention and influence in online news. We work with, and often get inspired by other data-centered and visualization projects. Using a Media Cloud search to gather my data, I set out to create this quick visualization and further the conversation on how our news media discusses facial recognition.

The visualization above shows the top 20 words used in close proximity to phrases like "facial recognition" in frout different countries. The data is soured from Media Cloud's collections of national media sources for India, Nigeria, the UK and the US. This content was filtered to include only English-language articles between January of 2019 and March of 2020. Keywords matching the search phrases were removed. Rollover a word to highlight where it shows up on each country's list.

Initial Observations

This is just a quick sketch to compare online news reporting to the Density Design Lab's social media results. A real investigation would require more time and a dive into the data. However, this quick visualization already reveals some interesting similarities and differences. Below are three things worth highlighting, each presented with a miniature version of the visualization to give some context for where the mentioned words show up.

Big Tech Worries

Not surprisingly Facebook, Amazon and Google are central to online news reports about facial recognition. While in the Density Design Lab data Alibaba shows up as a top social media term in both China and US social media data, it barely registers in our online news data.

Privacy is Paramount

This shows up as a top term in all four of our countries, matching the social media data from the Density Design Lab. Clearly the question of facial recognition is closely tied to privacy concerns in both online news and social media posts.

Social Good?

Our news data includes reporting on multiple purposes of facial recognition - “id”, “unlock”, “authentication”, “railway”, “iPhone”. However the Density Design Lab data also includes “security check” and “police force”, and “fight crime” - a narrative prevalent in social media that seems missing in the news reporting.
