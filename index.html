Facial Recognition in the News

the top terms used to talk about facial recognition in english language news sources in 2019

By: Rahul Bhargava

At the MIT Center for Civic Media I work on Media Cloud, a set of tools and processes for studying attention and influence in online news. We work with, and often get inspired by other data-centered and visualization projects. I recently came across The Density Design lab of the Politecnico Milan, who do wonderful work researching and teaching how to visualize complex phenomenon.

A recent project in their Final Synthesis Design Studio course created an interesting visual, and produced some inital findings, on how different people on global social media networks discuss focused on facial recognition. My collegue Joy Buolamwini, PhD student in our group, founded the Algorithmic Justice League and does soe of the most important early work on this topic, so I was interested to read their findings.

Their 2nd research question was particularly interesting to me: "In which terms are people talking about facial recognition on the most popular social media platforms in U.S. (and other English speaking countries), China, Italy and India?" Inspired by their work researching and visualizing this, I decided to look at how facial recognition was covered in English-language online news in various countries.

The diagram above shows the top words used in close proximity to phrases like "facial recognition". Each column represents the top 20 words from a different country's English language news media online. Rollover a word to highlight where it shows up on each country's list.
